Vowel pronunciation is one of the major contributors to the difference in English accents. This map illustrates some differences in vowel pronunciation in Louisiana state, based on the data collected by Quintrelé Jones and Irina Shport in 2018-2019. Shport and Johnson compared vowels in African-American residents of New Iberia, New Orleans, and the speakers who relocated from New Orleans to New Iberia after Hurricane Katrina in 2005. In addition, data from European-American residents of New Iberia were also recorded. Audio files on the current map are available only for speakers LK (female, African-American), LJ (male, African-American), AC (female, European-American), BC (male, European-American) (circled in red).

How to use this map:

- Select a vowel on the right.

- Click on a point to see speaker’s demographic information and listen to selected vowel pronunciation in words.
